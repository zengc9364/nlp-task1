Computers have evolved from room-sized machines to portable devices that fit in our pockets, reshaping nearly every aspect of modern life. Early computers relied on vacuum tubes and mechanical parts, performing simple calculations at slow speeds. Today’s systems use advanced microprocessors, multi-core CPUs, and high-speed memory to handle complex tasks such as artificial intelligence, data analysis, and real-time simulation. Software, from operating systems to specialized applications, determines how we interact with these machines. The internet has connected billions of computers worldwide, enabling instant communication, cloud computing, and global collaboration. As technology advances, computers continue to become more powerful, energy-efficient, and intelligent, supporting innovations in science, medicine, engineering, and entertainment. They are no longer just tools but essential partners in human progress, driving the digital transformation of societies across the globe
